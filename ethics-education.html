▸ MISSION BRIEFING - THE PROBLEM
Why this ethics course was created.
In 2022, Pennsylvania started requiring all educator preparation, induction, and professional development programs to teach professional ethics. These competencies must be fully integrated statewide by 2023–24 for professional development and by 2024–25 for preparation and induction programs. Before this change, ethics content at Commonwealth University–Bloomsburg was spread across multiple courses with no consistent structure or alignment to PDE standards. Creating this course gives the material a dedicated home, ensures compliance with state law, and provides future teachers with a strong, consistent foundation in ethical decision‑making.
⚠ ACTIVE DEBUFFS ON PARTY
DEBUFF · 01
No centralized home for ethics instruction.
DEBUFF · 02

DEBUFF · 03
No consistent delivery across the program.
◆ STRATEGY - THE SOLUTION
CHOSEN APPROACH
A self-paced module where learners decide, not just watch.
A scenario-driven eLearning module hosted in Brightspace. Three modules, one mastery test, Vyond-animated videos dropping learners into real classroom dilemmas - and auto-graded everything so it scales without burning out the professor.

Built backward from a single goal.

STAGE 05 · EVALUATION
Formative + summative, grounded in Kirkpatrick.
Our evaluation strategy was multi-dimensional, utilizing both formative and summative components to ensure the module's instructional integrity. We conducted rigorous formative evaluations through one-to-one sessions with subject matter experts and small group trials with five graduate students, which directly led to the refinement of ambiguous scenarios and navigation instructions.
For long-term impact, we grounded our summative assessment in Kirkpatrick's four-level model. This allowed us to track immediate learner reactions and knowledge acquisition, while also establishing frameworks for observing behavioral shifts in field experiences and measuring program-level success at the Department of Education level.
▸ EVALUATION METRICS
Tracking Kirkpatrick’s Level 1 Reaction data through Brightspace analytics and completion rates.
▸ FORMATIVE
↳ 1-to-1 SME review - ambiguous scenario question rewritten, change sensitive wordings to more flexible ones
↳ Small group trial (5 grad students) - drag-and-drop instructions clarified, UI improvements
▸ SUMMATIVE - KIRKPATRICK MODEL
↳ Level 1 (Reaction) — LMS analytics: completion rates, video viewing, time-on-task, and performance on embedded quizzes.
↳ Level 2 (Learning) — Cumulative auto-graded final exam across all 3 modules; 80% required to pass.
↳ Level 3 (Behavior) — Structured observation checklists and reflection prompts in subsequent coursework and field experiences.
↳ Level 4 (Results) — Aggregate program-level data to identify strengths, areas of difficulty, and opportunities for curricular refinement at the Department of Education level.
